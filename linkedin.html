LinkedIn Campaign

Three posts built around Unreached

A three-post LinkedIn sequence tied to the Unreached report and reported feature, timed to National Minority Health Month and National Public Health Week.

01
Monday 6 April · 7:30 AM CT

"She did not walk in. She logged on."

LinkedIn copy

There are roughly 20,000 Native American women Veterans in the United States. They serve at higher rates than women in any other demographic group.

Only twenty-eight studies have ever been published on their health.

We spent time with a women Veterans peer support group run through the Arizona Department of Veterans’ Services, reaching women in Navajo County, Apache County, and homebound Veterans with no other route to peer support.

This is where the story starts.

Notes

The human story opens the week. The quote lands without context, which is the point: VA and tribal health contacts will recognize it before they finish the sentence. Most people scrolling on a Monday morning will understand it before they register what program it’s about.

Link to the article in the first comment. 7:30 AM CT catches the professional scroll before the workday closes in.

@AZ Dept of Veterans’ Services #NationalMinorityHealthMonth #WomenVeterans #HealthEquity #NavajoNation #SocialIsolation
02
Wednesday 8 April · 7:30 AM CT

"The distrust is not irrational."

LinkedIn copy

The Phoenix Indian School operated in Arizona from 1891 to 1990. For nearly a century, Diné children were separated from their families, forbidden from speaking their language, stripped of the clan names through which a person knows who they belong to.

The VA is a federal institution.

When we talk about why Native American communities are underserved by healthcare systems, we often frame it as a logistics problem. Distance. Bandwidth. Access.

It is also a history problem.

We wrote about this, and about the women Veterans peer support program trying to change it, for National Minority Health Month.

Notes

Shifts the frame from logistics to history. Different day, different audience: tribal health directors, HHS officers, health equity researchers. The sentence lands not because it’s surprising, but because few organizations with direct involvement state it this plainly.

Deliberately pointed at federal healthcare. The audience it might unsettle is not this audience, and softening the copy to avoid it would undercut the whole point.
#NationalMinorityHealthMonth #HealthEquity #NativeAmericanHealth #VeteransHealth #TribalHealth #SocialDeterminantsOfHealth #SeniorHealth
03
Friday 10 April · 9 AM CT · Report launch

28 studies. 20,000 veterans.

LinkedIn copy

Twenty-eight studies.

That is how many peer-reviewed publications, from a screen of 1,500, met the inclusion criteria when researchers in 2024 tried to understand the health of Native American women Veterans.

There are approximately 20,000 of them in the United States. They serve at higher rates than women of any other demographic group. They have done so for over two centuries.

Twenty-eight studies.

Today we’re releasing State of Social Isolation 2026, a free ungated review of what the research says about loneliness, health outcomes, and what helps.

Notes

Report launch day. The ratio lands before any headline does. Researchers and grant reviewers will stop on this one.

Goes live the same morning as the report page. Link to the report in the first comment, not the article.

The copy opens and closes on "Twenty-eight studies." That repetition is intentional. It should stay.
